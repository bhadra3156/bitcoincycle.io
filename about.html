A deep-dive into the expansion/compression framework powering the predictions on BitcoinCycle.io — what it is, why it works, and its limitations.

What Is the 1064/364 Model?

The 1064/364 model is a time-based cycle framework that divides Bitcoin's market behaviour into two distinct phases:

Expansion Phase — 1,064 days
The period from a market trough (All-Time Low, ATL) to a market peak (All-Time High, ATH). This is the bull phase: accumulation, markup, and euphoria. It lasts exactly 1,064 calendar days — roughly 2 years and 11 months.

Compression Phase — 364 days
The period from a peak (ATH) back to a trough (ATL). This is the bear phase: distribution, markdown, and capitulation. It lasts exactly 364 calendar days — virtually one calendar year.

Together, one full cycle spans 1,428 days (~3.91 years). The model predicts that these phases repeat with metronomic regularity, driven by Bitcoin's 4-year halving schedule and market psychology.

Why the Nov 2022 FTX Bottom Is the Anchor

Every cycle model needs an anchor — a starting point with high historical confidence. We use 21 November 2022 because it represents the capitulation low following the FTX exchange collapse, one of the most severe and unambiguous selling events in Bitcoin's history.

Why this date specifically? The FTX bottom (~$15,500) was a textbook structural low: maximum fear, maximum news coverage, maximum leverage liquidation. It satisfies the model's definition of an ATL — a point from which sustained accumulation begins.

Historical Basis

Bitcoin's price history supports a recurring cyclical pattern tied to its halving events (approximately every 210,000 blocks, ~4 years). While the exact day count has varied in historical cycles, the 1064/364 model provides a clean mathematical approximation of the dominant pattern.

Key Observations

Halving correlation: Bitcoin halvings occur roughly every 1,458 days. The 1,428-day model cycle sits within ~2% of this interval — suggesting the cycle is fundamentally supply-driven.

Asymmetry: Bull markets last ~3x longer than bear markets (1,064 vs 364 days). This mirrors human psychology: markets take stairs up and elevators down, but Bitcoin's bear phases are notably swift relative to its accumulation phases.

Predictive consistency: When backtested against the 2018–2019 bear market and the 2020–2021 bull cycle, the model produces date estimates within weeks of actual major turning points.

What This Model Is NOT

This model does not predict price levels — only timing. It does not incorporate on-chain metrics, macroeconomic conditions, regulatory changes, ETF flows, or geopolitical events. It is a temporal skeleton, not a complete market forecast.
