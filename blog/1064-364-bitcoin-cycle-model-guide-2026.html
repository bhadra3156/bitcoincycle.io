What Is the 1064/364 Cycle Model?

The 1064/364 Bitcoin Cycle Model is an algorithmic framework that divides Bitcoin's price history into repeating phases:

  • 1,064-day Expansion Phase — the bull run, from cycle bottom to cycle top
  • 364-day Compression Phase — the bear market, from cycle top back to the next bottom

Together these form a full cycle of approximately 1,428 days (~3.9 years), closely mirroring Bitcoin's 4-year halving rhythm.

📌 Key anchor point: The November 2022 FTX collapse bottom (~$15,500) marks the start of Cycle 4's expansion phase.

Why the FTX Bottom Anchors Cycle 4

The November 2022 crash to ~$15,500 was the most structurally significant capitulation event since Bitcoin's early years. Unlike previous bear market lows, this bottom was:

  • Triggered by a black swan exchange collapse (FTX)
  • Accompanied by record high open interest liquidation
  • Followed by sustained accumulation by long-term holders

This makes it the definitive anchor for Cycle 4 under the 1064/364 model.

10 Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is the 1064/364 model the same as the halving cycle?

They are correlated but not identical. The halving occurs every ~1,458 days; the 1064/364 model totals ~1,428 days. The halving acts as a catalyst within the cycle, not the cycle itself.

2. What defines a cycle "bottom"?

A cycle bottom is the lowest closing price between two consecutive market tops, confirmed by on-chain accumulation and reduced exchange supply.

3. Has the model ever been wrong?

No model is perfect. Cycle 3's top came slightly early relative to pure 1064-day counting from the 2018 bottom. The model provides a probabilistic range, not an exact date.

4. Does the model work on all timeframes?

It is designed for macro (multi-year) analysis only. It should not be used for day-trading or short-term entries.

5. What happens to the cycle as Bitcoin matures?

As institutional adoption grows, cycle volatility is expected to decrease, and cycle lengths may gradually extend as the market deepens.

6. How do halvings interact with the 1064/364 model?

Halvings typically occur in the middle of the expansion phase (~500–600 days in), acting as a mid-cycle accelerant rather than a starting gun.

7. Should I buy at the model's predicted bottom?

The model is an educational framework. Always do your own research and consider dollar-cost averaging rather than attempting to time exact lows.

8. What price does the model predict for Cycle 4's top?

The model predicts timing, not price targets. Price targets require separate valuation models (stock-to-flow, power law, etc.).

9. Is this based on Satoshi's original design?

No. The 1064/364 pattern emerged empirically from historical price data. It is an observed regularity, not an engineered feature.
