Bitcoin Cycle Explained: Expansion vs Compression

📅 May 5, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read ✍️ BitcoinCycle.io

If you've been in crypto for any length of time, you've heard the phrase "Bitcoin cycle." But what does it actually mean — and more importantly, how can you use it? This guide breaks it down simply, even if you've never traded a single satoshi.

What Is a Bitcoin Cycle?

A Bitcoin cycle is a repeating pattern in which Bitcoin's price goes through two distinct phases: a long expansion (bull market) and a shorter compression (bear market). These phases have repeated with remarkable consistency since Bitcoin's early days.

The 1064/364 model defines these phases precisely:

Expansion Phase
1,064
Days of bull market — from the cycle low (ATL) to the cycle high (ATH)
Compression Phase
364
Days of bear market — from the cycle high (ATH) back to the next cycle low (ATL)

Together these form a full cycle of 1,428 days — roughly 3 years and 11 months. The current cycle is anchored to the 21 November 2022 FTX bottom at ~$15,500.

The Expansion Phase (Bull Market)

The expansion phase is what most people think of as the "Bitcoin bull run." It starts from the cycle's lowest point (ATL) and ends at its highest point (ATH). During this phase:

  • Demand steadily outpaces new supply
  • Retail and institutional interest increases
  • Prices make higher highs and higher lows
  • Media coverage intensifies as price rises
  • A halving event typically occurs mid-expansion, acting as a catalyst

📈 In the current cycle: The expansion phase started 21 Nov 2022 and lasted 1,064 days, with the model predicting a cycle top around 19 Oct 2025. Bitcoin reached approximately $126,200 on 6 Oct 2025, closely matching this window.

The Compression Phase (Bear Market)

The compression phase follows the ATH. It's shorter — only 364 days — but psychologically brutal. During this phase:

  • Prices retrace significantly from the ATH
  • Weaker hands sell, long-term holders accumulate
  • Media coverage turns negative
  • Exchange supply drops as holders move coins to cold storage
  • The market "compresses" in preparation for the next expansion

📉 In the current cycle: The compression phase is projected to run from ~Oct 2025 to ~Oct 2026, with the next ATL (cycle low) predicted around 19 October 2026 — which would mark the start of Cycle 5.

Why Does the Cycle Repeat?

The cycle repeats because of a combination of structural and psychological forces:

1. Bitcoin's Supply Schedule

Every ~4 years, Bitcoin's block reward halves. This reduces the rate of new supply entering the market. When demand stays constant or rises but supply slows, prices tend to increase — feeding the expansion phase.

2. Market Psychology

Human emotions — fear and greed — drive predictable patterns. Greed drives prices to extremes during expansion; fear and capitulation push prices to extremes during compression. These emotional extremes create the ATH and ATL that anchor each cycle.

3. Liquidity Cycles

Macro liquidity conditions — central bank policy, credit expansion, risk appetite — move in multi-year cycles that often sync with Bitcoin's pattern.

How to Use the Cycle Model

The 1064/364 model is a macro framework, not a trading signal. Here's how to apply it sensibly:

  • Early expansion: Historically the best time to accumulate Bitcoin for long-term holders
  • Mid expansion: After the halving, the pace of price increase often accelerates
  • Late expansion: Risk increases; the cycle top is approaching
  • Compression: Patience pays — this phase ends and a new expansion begins

⚠️ Important: No model is perfect. Use the 1064/364 cycle as one tool among many. Always consider position sizing, your own risk tolerance, and never invest more than you can afford to lose.
