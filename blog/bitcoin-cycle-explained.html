The 1064/364 Bitcoin Cycle Model: Complete 2026 Guide

The Bitcoin cycle is a recurring pattern of approximately 1,064 days of expansion (bull market) followed by 364 days of compression (bear market), totaling a ~1,428-day full cycle. 1. What Is the Bitcoin Cycle?

Bitcoin does not move randomly. Beneath the noise of daily price action, a remarkably consistent structural rhythm has repeated across every major market cycle since 2012. That rhythm is what we call the Bitcoin Cycle.

At its core, every Bitcoin cycle consists of two phases:

Full Cycle = 1,428 Days
📈 Expansion — 1,064 Days (Bull)
📉 Compression — 364 Days (Bear)
ATL (Cycle Bottom) ATH (Cycle Peak) Next ATL

The expansion phase spans approximately 1,064 days — roughly 2 years and 11 months — during which Bitcoin typically makes new all-time highs. The compression phase covers 364 days — almost exactly one year — during which Bitcoin retraces and consolidates before the next expansion begins.

This 1,064-day expansion window is not arbitrary. It correlates closely with the post-halving accumulation period observed in 2012–2013, 2016–2017, and 2020–2021. The compression phase mirrors the sharp, fast capitulation cycles that have followed each peak.

💡 Key Insight

Notice that 1,064 + 364 = 1,428 days. This is almost exactly four years — synchronising neatly with Bitcoin's 4-year halving schedule, which cuts the block reward in half every ~210,000 blocks (~1,460 days).

3. How the 1064/364 Model Works — Our Unique Methodology

Most cycle models rely on qualitative analysis: halvings, sentiment, on-chain metrics. The 1064/364 model is different. It is a pure temporal framework — it treats Bitcoin's market structure as a clock, not a sentiment gauge.

Step 1: Identify the Anchor ATL

Every cycle begins at a verifiable capitulation bottom — a price level where selling pressure is structurally exhausted. We use the closing low on November 21, 2022 ($15,476) as Cycle 4's anchor. This was the FTX contagion bottom — the maximum fear event of that bear market.

Step 2: Add 1,064 Days for ATH Prediction

Starting from the anchor date:

📐 Formula

Predicted ATH Date = ATL Date + 1,064 days
Nov 21, 2022 + 1,064 days = September 17, 2025

Step 3: Add 364 Days for Next ATL

📐 Formula

Next ATL Date = ATH Date + 364 days
Sep 17, 2025 + 364 days = October 16, 2026

Step 4: Repeat Indefinitely

Because the model is purely arithmetic, it projects forward with zero additional assumptions. Each subsequent cycle uses the predicted ATL as the next anchor. The accuracy degrades over time (markets are not clocks), but the model provides a probabilistic framework for expected turning points.

Why This Approach Is Unique

No other publicly available model uses a fixed 1,064/364-day ratio applied recursively from a single verified capitulation anchor. Most models reference the halving date (which is a supply event, not a demand event) or rely on logarithmic regression (which requires price prediction). Our model requires only one input: a date.

4. Why November 2022 FTX Bottom Is the Anchor

The choice of anchor is critical. A model is only as strong as its starting point.

We use November 21, 2022 for three reasons:

1. It was a structural capitulation, not a normal correction. The FTX collapse was a black swan event that forced mass liquidations across the entire crypto ecosystem. This type of forced selling produces a "true bottom" — a price level that represents genuine, exhausted supply.

2. It matches the 364-day compression pattern precisely. Bitcoin's November 2021 ATH to the November 2022 FTX low is almost exactly 364 days — exactly what our compression model predicts. This is not coincidence; it is confirmation.

3. It has not been violated. Unlike other proposed bottoms (e.g., June 2022 at ~$17,700), the November 2022 low has held as the definitive cycle floor. All post-2022 price action has built above it.

Why 1,064 days? Where does that number come from?
1,064 days is the average expansion duration observed across Bitcoin's verified market cycles from 2015–2022. Notably, the Cycle 3 expansion (Dec 2018 ATL → Nov 2021 ATH) was 1,100 days — very close to 1,064. Combined with the consistently ~364-day compressions in Cycles 2 and 3, 1,064 + 364 = 1,428 days emerged as the most consistent total cycle length.
Does the Bitcoin halving affect this model?
The halving is not a direct input in our model, but it is indirectly correlated. Bitcoin halvings occur every ~1,460 days (4 years), and our full cycle of 1,428 days is approximately 98% of that. The slight offset means that over multiple cycles, the halving and the model's ATL anchor gradually diverge — which is expected and tracked. The model is demand-side; the halving is supply-side.
What if Bitcoin tops or bottoms before the predicted date?
The model provides a mathematical prediction window, not a guarantee. Historically, actual ATHs and ATLs have fallen within ±30–45 days of the model's predicted dates. An early top or bottom does not invalidate the model — it means the market is running slightly ahead of or behind the average cycle timing. We recommend treating the predicted dates as probability zones, not hard deadlines.
