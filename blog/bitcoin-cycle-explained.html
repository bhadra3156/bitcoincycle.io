The 1064/364 Bitcoin Cycle Model: Complete 2026 Guide

The definitive breakdown of Bitcoin's 1,064-day expansion and 364-day compression model. Covers every historical cycle from 2012–2022, why the Nov 2022 FTX bottom anchors Cycle 4, full predictions through 2050, and a 10-question FAQ.

🔍 What Is the 1064/364 Model?

The 1064/364 model is a framework for understanding Bitcoin's recurring market structure. It divides every Bitcoin cycle into two distinct phases:

Expansion Phase (ATL → ATH): 1,064 days — the bull market from cycle low to cycle high.

Compression Phase (ATH → ATL): 364 days — the bear market from cycle high to the next cycle low.

Full Cycle: 1,428 days, approximately 3 years and 11 months.

Unlike the popular 4-year halving cycle thesis, the 1064/364 model is anchored to market structure rather than supply events. The current cycle (Cycle 4) is anchored to the 21 November 2022 FTX crash bottom — one of the most clearly defined macro lows in Bitcoin's history.

📌 Why the Nov 2022 FTX Bottom?

The 21 November 2022 low (~$15,500) was caused by the collapse of the FTX exchange — a black swan event that wiped out billions in market value within days. While extreme, this low displayed all the hallmarks of a macro cycle bottom: extreme fear, maximum capitulation, and a sharp volume spike followed by a sustained recovery.

Using this as the Cycle 4 anchor produces highly aligned predictions with the model's historical averages, making it the natural starting point.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: What exactly is the 1064/364 model?

It's a Bitcoin cycle prediction framework using 1,064 days for the bull market expansion phase (ATL to ATH) and 364 days for the bear market compression phase (ATH to ATL). These averages are derived from Cycles 2 and 3.

Q2: Why is it anchored to November 2022 and not the 2024 halving?

The model is anchored to market structure — specifically the clearest macro low in recent history. The halving is a supply event, not a market bottom. Bitcoin hit an ATH before the 2024 halving, suggesting the halving cycle hypothesis has decoupled from real market behaviour.

Q3: When is the next Bitcoin ATH predicted?

Cycle 4's ATH was predicted on ~17 September 2025 (actual: ~6 Oct 2025 at ~$126,200). The next ATH (Cycle 5) is predicted on approximately 17 August 2029.

Q4: When is the next Bitcoin ATL predicted?

The next predicted cycle low is 19 October 2026 — the end of the current compression phase and the start of Cycle 5's expansion.

Q5: How accurate has the model been historically?

Cycles 2 and 3 had expansion durations of 1,067 and 1,061 days respectively — both within 3 days of the 1,064-day average. The compression durations were 363 and 376 days. The model is a mathematical average, not a guarantee.

Q6: How long is a full Bitcoin cycle?

According to the model, a full cycle is 1,428 days — roughly 3 years and 11 months. This covers one expansion and one compression phase.

Q7: Is this the same as the 4-year cycle?

Similar but distinct. The 4-year halving cycle aligns Bitcoin cycles with block reward halvings (~1,460 days). The 1064/364 model uses observed market structure and averages 1,428 days per cycle — about 32 days shorter than a 4-year cycle.

Q8: Does this account for the 2024 halving?

The model does not use the halving as an input. However, the 2024 halving (April 2024) fell within the Cycle 4 expansion phase, consistent with the historical pattern of halvings occurring mid-expansion.

Q9: Will the cycle get shorter over time as Bitcoin matures?

Possibly. As Bitcoin's market cap grows and liquidity deepens, cycles may compress. The model will need updating if future cycle data diverges materially from the 1064/364 averages. We monitor this on the dashboard.

Q10: Is this financial advice?

No. The 1064/364 model is a mathematical framework for educational purposes only. It is not a guarantee of future performance. Always conduct your own research and consult a qualified financial professional before making investment decisions.
