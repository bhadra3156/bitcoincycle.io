Does the Bitcoin Halving Still Drive the Cycle?

📅 May 5, 2026 ⏱ 10 min read ✍️ BitcoinCycle.io

For years, the dominant narrative in Bitcoin was simple: the 4-year halving cycle drives everything. Every ~4 years, the block reward halves, supply shock follows, and price explodes. But in 2024, something unexpected happened — Bitcoin hit a new all-time high before the halving. Has the model broken down? Or is something deeper at work?

The Traditional Halving Narrative

The classic halving cycle theory goes like this:

  • Bitcoin's block reward halves every ~210,000 blocks (~4 years)
  • This reduces daily new supply entering the market
  • If demand stays constant, reduced supply pushes prices up
  • The ATH arrives roughly 12–18 months after each halving
  • A bear market follows, resetting for the next cycle

This framework worked remarkably well for three cycles:

Halving Date Pre-Halving Price Cycle ATH ATH Date
Halving 1 Nov 2012 ~$12 ~$1,150 Nov 2013
Halving 2 Jul 2016 ~$650 ~$19,800 Dec 2017
Halving 3 May 2020 ~$8,700 ~$69,000 Nov 2021
Halving 4 Apr 2024 ~$63,000 ~$126,200 Oct 2025

🚨 The anomaly: For Halving 4 (April 2024), Bitcoin was already at ~$63,000 — above its previous cycle ATH of $19,800 — before the halving even occurred. The old "buy before the halving" playbook had already played out.

Why Did the 2024 Cycle Break the Old Pattern?

Several structural changes explain why Cycle 4 behaved differently:

1. Spot Bitcoin ETFs (January 2024)

The approval of US Spot Bitcoin ETFs in January 2024 brought unprecedented institutional demand before the halving. BlackRock's IBIT, Fidelity's FBTC, and others absorbed enormous sell-side pressure, front-running the supply shock. Demand arrived early; price followed.

2. Market Maturity and Pre-Positioning

With each cycle, more sophisticated participants enter the market. By 2023–2024, institutions, hedge funds, and algorithmic traders had fully modelled the halving cycle. They bought early — compressing the pre-halving accumulation phase and front-running the traditional post-halving rally.

3. Macro Liquidity Environment

The Federal Reserve's rate-cut signals in late 2023 and 2024 injected risk appetite into markets. Bitcoin, now widely traded as a macro asset, responded to liquidity conditions independently of its own supply schedule.

So Is the Halving Irrelevant?

Not exactly. The halving still matters — but as a mid-cycle accelerant, not a starting gun.

In the 1064/364 model, the halving typically occurs around day 500–600 of the expansion phase. It acts as fuel already in the engine, not the ignition key. The real ignition is the cycle low — the moment of maximum fear and capitulation where smart money begins accumulating.

✅ Verdict: The halving is not dead — but it no longer defines the cycle timing on its own. The 1064/364 model, anchored to market structure rather than supply events, provides a more accurate and front-running-resistant framework for cycle prediction.

What This Means for Cycle 5 and Beyond

If the 1064/364 model holds, the next cycle low (ATL) is projected around 19 October 2026 — marking the start of Cycle 5's expansion phase. The next halving (~2028) would then fall mid-expansion, again acting as an accelerant rather than a trigger.

As Bitcoin matures, adoption deepens, and more sophisticated capital enters, the 1064/364 model's market-structure anchor is likely to remain more reliable than the halving-date anchor.
